Jackson Michaels

Machine Learning & Multi-Modal Fusion Researcher | PhD Student at University of Massachusetts Amherst

I am a PhD student at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where my research focuses on the robustness and interpretability of multi-modal machine learning models. My work investigates how systems integrate vision, language, and audio, building on my Master's degree from Northwestern University. There, my thesis centered on text-to-audio generation using diffusion models, and I contributed to bio-inspired computer vision research by developing pipelines to track features in noisy X-ray data. My academic background is supplemented by industry experience in computer vision and software development at Bosch.

Arachnid Eye Tracking Toolkit

Comprehensive image and path processing toolkit for tracking spider eye tubes from X-ray video data. Features video processing with advanced denoising, Fourier filtering, and temporal analysis, plus sophisticated path cleaning with Kalman filtering, jump detection, and shape matching algorithms. Collaboration between Northwestern's Bio Inspired Vision Lab and University of Cincinnati's Morehouse Lab.
